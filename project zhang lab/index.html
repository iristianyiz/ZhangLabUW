Research

"Our research is geared toward developing materials and devices for biological and medical applications, and can be broadly categorized in three directions and conducted in three labs within the group."

Nanoparticle Lab

Focuses its research on cancer diagnosis and treatment through imaging enhancement and targeted and controlled therapeutic payload delivery.

Tissue Engineering Lab

Concentrates on development of biocompatible materials serving as biodegradable scaffolds and/or drug delivery depots.

Biosensor Lab

Targets the detection and identification of chemical and biological agents and development of drug screening techniques.

Xiaojie Lin

Acting Instructor

Xiaojie is an acting instructor in the Department of MSE at the University of Washington. Holding a Ph.D. in materials engineering from the University of Tokyo since 2014, he possesses extensive expertise in the preparation of both natural and synthetic biocompatible polymers. His remarkable skills lie in applying these materials for the surface coating of medical devices, tissue regeneration, cell therapy, drug delivery, biosensors/biochips, and protein-polymer conjugates.

Lab Highlights

Tiny structure Big impact

Using chitosan to deliver anti-cancer drugs

University of Washington researchers have developed a targeted nanoparticle that kills cancer cells by simultaneously delivering chemotherapeutic drugs and activating the immune system. Zhang uses chitosan to build nanoparticles — tiny structures barely thousandths of a hair-width in size, too small to be seen with a conventional microscope — for use in cancer therapy.
